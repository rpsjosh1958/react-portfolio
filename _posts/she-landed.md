Somewhere in 2009, when I was eight years old, my mother landed in Ghana on a Sunday. Because my parents were moving back permanently, they brought a lot of stuff. So much stuff. Too much stuff, honestly.

I hadn't met her before; we had only ever spoken on the phone, and she used to send me things. When she arrived, I actually thought she was really cool. She was nice, and she'd tell me to kiss her on the cheeks. I remember thinking, *"Okay, maybe I can live with this."*

Right off the bat, she wanted us to go to the beach. But to get there, we had to pass by the church. My dad immediately shut it down, worried about what people would say if they saw us skipping out. *"People might see us,"* and all that.

Before long, the dynamic in the house shifted completely. My mom didn't get along with the house help, so the help and her kids were let go. Suddenly, it was just the three of us. We looked like this perfect, happy family from the outside. We'd even meet up with Mummy and my cousins, and we'd all go to the beach together. It was cool.

But over time, the house started filling up with other people. My parents brought in different kids to stay with us — some older than me, some my age. But eventually, they all had to leave, and that's when everything fell squarely on me. I became responsible for doing everything around the house. My mom was always busy. My dad ran a school, so she served as the administrator for a while. Later, she opened a bakery at my grandma's house, which she eventually had to move directly into our home after thieves broke into the house. We needed someone around the place at all times.

Meanwhile, I was still going to Danem, moving into grade four or five. And that's when I did something crazy that got me kicked out for good.

I wanted a phone so badly that I stole money to get one. When my parents found out and confronted me, I went completely in circles trying to lie my way out of it. It didn't work. I was forced to leave Danem. I went to a local school right around my area for about a week or two before finally moving to Crown and Glory to continue my education.

The wildest part? my auntie(Mummy) didn't even know the real story. All she was told was that my parents were moving me to a different school. To this day, I don't even think she knows what actually went down.
