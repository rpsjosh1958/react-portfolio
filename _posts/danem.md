So, after the great garage mix-up where I realized my aunt wasn't actually my biological mother (but the first person I'd ever call "Mummy"), life clicked into a brand-new gear. Literally the next day, I started school.

Danem Royal, a school right in the neighborhood.

Looking back, Danem was where the foundation of my life really started to set. I met great people there — childhood friends who eventually just became family friends. Honestly? Those were incredibly fun times. I still remember the morning car rides with my auntie from home to school. We had a whole routine. We'd stop to get snacks, and more often than not, we had to stop for new stationery because I had an absolute superpower for misplacing my pens and pencils.

I was also a massive bookworm back then. Danem had this weekly activity where they'd give you a book for the weekend, and you had to read it and write a report. I used to do all of that with my new family — my auntie, my cousins, and my uncle all pitching in.

Then came the UCMAS classes. If you know, you know. It started as a club abacus thing at Danem, but my auntie noticed I was into it, so she upgraded me. Every Saturday morning, she'd pack my breakfast in a cup and drop me off at the private UCMAS center in Labone. While I was busy doing mental math like a mini-calculator, she'd head over to my grandma's place nearby, wait for me to close, and then come pick me up. If I was lucky, the ride home included ice cream. We'd head back, eat, and just vibe. I went as far as getting to compete in the National UCMAS competition at Burma Camp but couldn't stay to the end of the competition.

Birthdays as a kid were another level of fun. My family would package up little treats for me to take to school so I could celebrate with my classmates.

And the outfits? Man, I was styling. I don't know where my auntie got them from, but she got me these matching shirt-and-short sets that I absolutely loved. I had them in brown, blue, and an orange one. I felt fly in those sets.

Being a kid there was great. It was a beautiful, smooth ride... right up until I did something completely crazy and had to leave Danem and move in with my actual people who adopted me.

But hey, that's a story for the next piece.
